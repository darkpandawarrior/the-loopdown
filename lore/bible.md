# The Loopdown — Universe Bible

The through-line that makes the feed *bingeable*. Not a gimmick — a frame that turns
scattered technical posts into a serialized world people follow. Distilled from the
author's fiction (`archive/the-loopdown-story.md`, `deadline.md`, `ctc-cost-to-company.md`),
which already personifies abstractions and builds worlds to carry real ideas.

## The premise

> An engineer is stuck in a loop. Same broken Wednesday, over and over — 52 iterations
> and counting. Each pass through, they meet the same bugs, the same systems that lie,
> the same 3 a.m. pages. And each pass, they understand a little more.
>
> **The Loopdown is the field notes from inside the loop.** Dispatches from someone who
> has seen this exact failure a thousand times and finally knows why it happens.

Why it works: **seniority *is* the loop.** A Lead isn't someone who's seen more code —
it's someone who's run the same failure enough times to recognize it on sight. The
metaphor is the message. (And it's literally the plot of the namesake story.)

## Canon rules

1. **The lesson is real; the lore is the wrapper.** The metaphor never bends the
   engineering. If a character's "personality" would misrepresent how the tech works,
   the tech wins. Always.
2. **First appearance = one-line origin.** The first time a character shows up, give it
   a single vivid line of introduction. After that, refer to it by name — regulars get
   the reward of recognition; newcomers still follow.
3. **Continuity is tracked.** `cast:` in each lesson's frontmatter records who appears.
   `build-registry.mjs` builds a Cast Appearances index. The canon is auditable.
4. **Consistent traits.** A character behaves the same way across every post. The
   Concussed Witness always lies *confidently*. Doze always locks the night shift.
5. **Warmth, never contempt.** Even the "villains" are sympathetic — they're doing
   their job, we just misunderstood them. (CancellationException isn't evil. It's a
   messenger everyone keeps killing.)
6. **Earn the callback.** Reference a past "episode" only when it genuinely connects.
   Forced continuity reads as try-hard; a real callback reads as a universe.

## Tone

Sharp senior engineer × playful teacher (see `voice/voice-profile.md`), now with a
recurring cast. Think: a naturalist's field journal, if the wildlife were race
conditions and the naturalist had strong opinions and a good sense of humor.

## The sign-off (recurring closer)

Rotate, don't repeat every time. These are the loop's signatures:

- *— filed from iteration N of the loop.*
- *The loop continues.*
- *See you next Wednesday.*
- *Another pass, another lesson.*

## What lives where

- `lore/cast.md` — the recurring characters (roster + traits + first appearance).
- `lore/series.md` — the arcs/series each pillar rolls up into.
- `voice/voice-profile.md` — *how* it's written. This file is *the world* it's written in.
